# NYTHOS Founder Script

Use this as the founder delivery script for the deck. The tone should stay calm, proof-first, and low-hype.

## Before you start

Open with confidence, but do not oversell. The strongest version of NYTHOS is:

`We are building a Base-first onchain intelligence product, proving demand through grants and paid pilots first, and only adding token access after there is product trust.`

## Slide 1 - Cover

Say:

NYTHOS is Base-first onchain intelligence for traders, builders, and communities. We already have a working beta, and the near-term goal is not a loud public token raise. The goal is to harden the product, win pilots, and earn the right to launch access infrastructure properly.

## Slide 2 - The problem

Say:

Most crypto users still have a bad intelligence stack. Explorers show raw data, social media gives delayed interpretation, and the best intelligence tools are priced for institutions. That leaves traders, communities, and small teams reacting late or paying too much.

## Slide 3 - The product

Say:

NYTHOS turns raw onchain activity into something usable. We detect whale-sized movements, score events by significance, track watched wallets, surface proof and history, and package the output in a dashboard, Telegram workflow, and API-ready layer. The point is not raw data volume. The point is decision-ready intelligence.

## Slide 4 - Why now

Say:

The market is ready for focused intelligence products. Onchain data is more available than ever, but interpretation is still weak for normal users and small teams. There is room for a lighter product that does one thing well instead of trying to be a full institutional terminal.

## Slide 5 - Why Base

Say:

Base is the right chain for NYTHOS because both the technical and commercial story fit there already. The contracts are configured for Base, the wallet experience is clean for a future access layer, and Base offers builder funding and distribution. It is much more credible for us to go deep on Base than to pretend to be everywhere at once.

## Slide 6 - Product proof

Say:

We only use proof we can defend. Today that means a working frontend beta, a live signal engine, founder-list and referral capture, Base-aligned contract setup, passing local smart-contract tests, and a passing production frontend build. We are not using inflated user numbers or artificial traction claims.

## Slide 7 - Business model

Say:

The first revenue wedge is custom intelligence pilots. We can sell monitored wallet flows, sharper signal feeds, and API-ready outputs to traders, Base communities, treasury teams, and builders who need focused intelligence without paying for a full terminal. That lets us monetize before we introduce token complexity.

## Slide 8 - Token strategy

Say:

The token stays in the long-term plan, but it is not the first story. The sequence is working beta, grants, paid pilots, external review, an optional small strategic round if needed, and only then Base deployment with token-gated access. That order is important because it turns the token into a credible extension of a product instead of a substitute for one.

## Slide 9 - The ask

Say:

The near-term ask is practical. We want Base Builder support, three to five paying pilots, and if needed a small strategic round in the twenty-five to fifty thousand dollar range after early proof. The funds go into product hardening, audit preparation, pilot acquisition, and runway.

## Slide 10 - Team and close

Say:

Right now we present publicly as the founding team, with identity available in direct diligence. The important point is that the build exists, the direction is disciplined, and the go-to-market path is realistic. NYTHOS is not trying to win with hype. NYTHOS is trying to become a real Base-native intelligence business first.

## Short Q&A Bridges

If someone asks why the token is not live yet:

Because token trust is easier to earn after product proof than before it.

If someone asks why Base instead of multi-chain first:

Because focused ecosystem fit gives us better funding, distribution, and credibility than a scattered launch story.

If someone asks what success looks like in the next 90 days:

Success is a cleaner founder demo, pilot conversions, better onboarding, grant momentum, and audit-ready Base deployment prep.
